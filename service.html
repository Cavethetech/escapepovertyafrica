Resources Specialization

A new context might require you to fundamentally diversify your resource base. How then do you tap into new financial resources and mobilize them to effectively meet your specific needs? Escape Poverty (EP) - Africa assists you with making strategic choices, implementing them and improving the capacities and essential fundraising skills of your staff.

The organization also provides Technical Assistance in the area of donor/partner engagement and relationship management, business opportunities identification and converting identified opportunities into businesses. We also provide TA in program/Project Design and Formulation, Concept notes and Proposals Development.

  • -We use the latest, proven methodologies and tools in an integrated manner.
  • -Our experts combine training and consultancy experiences from the field.

Program and Project Management

EP - Africa enables you to manage your programs and projects more effectively, while applying a participatory and strong results-oriented approach in all the phases of the project cycle. In close consultation with you, we choose the most appropriate tools to jointly develop your program or project proposals, (further) develop your monitoring system or carry out learning-oriented evaluations. We provide support throughout the entire project cycle and connect the different phases of the cycle to strengthen the learning, decision-making and accountability aspects of project management; We bring in experience in a wide range of participatory monitoring and evaluation tools; The whole process is results-oriented and at the same time it strengthens the team’s capacity and ownership of the products developed.



Monitoring and Evaluation

EP - Africa enables your (network) organization to capture, understand and learn from the results of your interventions. Together we design and implement a sound baseline and/or evaluation system that enable you to learn from past achievements to increase your future impact, whilst taking care of your accountability requirements. We cover your entire evaluation agenda, from baseline and mid-term to end-evaluation Our proven rigorous evaluation methods recognize and capture the non-linear and unpredictable aspects of social change We provide insight into the actual pathways towards social change and the significance of your contribution We go the extra mile in translating your evaluation findings into actionable steps for improvement



Agribusiness Development

Capacity Building of Start-up agribusinesses/SMEs to make them investment ready and facilitation of their access to finance by connecting them with impact investors, commercial finance and blended finance sources. EP-Africa also facilitates agricultural input and output market access for Smallholder farmers, Farmer Based Organizational and Cooperatives Development. Contract farming is also our specialty.



Youth Entrepreneurial Skills and Enterprises Development

EP-Africa economically empowers the youth particularly women by helping them develop entrepreneurial mindset and passion to start their own enterprises and further build the capacities of those enterprises to grow to become profitable, competitive and sustainable businesses.



Local Content and SMEs Supplier Development

EP-Africa provides Technical Assistance (TA) for large companies to effectively integrate SMEs into their main stream business supply chains. Our approach is a comprehensive demand and supply side business diagnosis study to identify available business opportunities and business constraints which informs the design and implementation of appropriate Local content and SMEs business linkages and supplier development program.



Business Development Services for MSMEs

Escape Poverty-Africa provides BDS services in the areas of SMEs Accounting and Financial Management, Business Planning, investment proposals development, training, business advice, business coaching and mentoring support to spur SMEs start-ups and growth.



Environmental and Economic Impact Assessment

EP-Africa provides Technical Assistance for large companies implementing large projects to understand the impacts of their business operations on the environment and their operational communities. Based on the outcome of environmental and economic impact Assessment study, policies, programs and projects are formulated to mitigate adverse effects of business operations on the environment and project impacted communities.



Transparency and Good Governance

Corruption is undoubtedly the most pressing governance and development challenge that Africa is confronted with today. Corruption has debilitating and corrosive effects on progress, stability and development of the continent. It impedes economic growth by discouraging foreign investments, creates distortion in resource allocation and competitive markets, increases the cost of doing business, and reduces the net-value of public spending. It also reduces the quality of services and public infrastructure and the volume of tax revenues, and encourages the misappropriation and misallocation of scarce resources. In the political realm, it undermines the rule of law, respect for human rights, accountability and transparency and weakens government institutions. This in turn erodes public legitimacy in government and compromises good governance. The social costs of corruption are also deleterious as it deepens income inequality, poverty and adversely affects good moral values in the society. In general, corruption is a challenge to sustainable economic development, peace and good governance.

While corruption is a global phenomenon, the impact is felt more in poor and underdeveloped countries (which Ghana is no exception), where resources for development are unduly diverted into private hands, which exacerbates poverty. According to the 2005 African Governance Report (AGR I), corruption is perceived as the most serious national problem next to poverty and unemployment.

In an effort to ensure transparency and good governance, Escape Poverty–Africa will work to build capacity of citizens, particularly women to participate in decision making processes and demand accountability from leaders and government officials. We believe that corruption will reduce if leaders and government officials become aware that the citizens are interested in their activities and they expect them to be accountable.
