ADVISORY BOARD


DR. MOSES EKOW OGOE: CHAIRMAN

A Doctoral Degree holder in Agricultural Extension (University of Cape Coast, Ghana). Also holds a Master of Philosophy Degree in Agricultural Extension - University of Cape Coast; B.Sc. Degree in Agriculture - Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, Kumasi Ghana; Postgraduate Diploma in Agricultural Administration - G.I.M.P.A., Ghana; and Certificate in Agriculture-related Information Processing - Hokkaido International Cooperation Agency, Obihiro, Japan.

A Management Board member of the Oil Palm Research Institute of the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research, Ghana.

Worked with the Ministry of Food and Agriculture for 13 years in various capacities as agricultural extension personnel.

A farmer with an estate of various agricultural commodities that have won him several awards including District Coconut Best Farmer for Ahanta West District (2001), Overall District Best farmer for Ahanta West District – 2011 and National Best Grasscutter Farmer – 2011. Adjudged the 2014 Overall Best Farmer for the Western Region of Ghana.

Worked for Ricerca e Cooperazione (an Italian International NGO) as Project Coordinator for the Southwestern and Central Regions of Ghana.

Provided a nine-month private agricultural land-use planning consultancy services to the Food and Agricultural Organization of the United Nations in the project “Ensuring farmers’ livelihoods and food security around Kakum Conservation Area” in the Central Region of Ghana.

Served for 3 years as the Program Manager of the Akyem branch of Opportunities Industrialization Centers International (OICI), and implemented Newmont Golden Ridge Limited's Mining for Sustainable Development program in the Birim North District of the Eastern Region of Ghana.

Energetic self-starter with very good analytical and organizational skills. Highly motivated, creative, versatile and possess skills at building effective and productive working relationships with stakeholders and staff.





BRIDGET OKYEREBEA MENYEH:SECRETARY

Bridget Okyerebea Menyeh is energy and environmental policy consultant and a current PhD researcher looking into household preferences for renewable energy investments in Sub-Saharan Africa. She holds a Master’s degree in Energy and the Environment from Lancaster University in the UK.

Bridget holds over six year’s experiences spanning marketing and public relations and renewable energy policy and research. Over the last two years, she has worked on a number of projects in the renewable energy and financing space including:

  • The Ghana Climate Innovation Centre project( Consultant at SNV Netherlands Development Organisation)

  • The EU-Horizon 2020 Crowdfundres Project (Assistant Coordinator at the University of Dundee)

  • The Benchmarking Study on Alternative Finance in Africa and the Middle East (Research Associate at the University of Cambridge Judge Business School)

  • Various projects with the Climate Bonds Initiative in London ( Research Intern)




SOLOMON ISAAC FAIBIL:MEMBER

Solomon is an experienced Chartered Accountant with over 12 years of expertise in international development and the private sector specifically in Grants Management and Operations, Financial Administration, Donor Accounting and Procurement. Solomon is well versed in multi-donor funded projects reporting and compliance requirements. He has provided strong technical assistance, and financial compliance audit and reviews for all of JA Africa’s Donors funded projects located in Botswana, Burkina-Faso, Gabon, Kenya, Mauritius, Nigeria, Senegal, South Africa, Swaziland, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe and JA Africa Regional Operating Center while working as Regional Finance Officer and Regional Grants and Finance Manager for JA Africa.

Before joining JA Africa, Solomon worked as Head of Finance for Organization for Livelihood Enhancement Services (OLIVES) - a non-governmental, not-for-profit making organization. Prior to this, Solomon worked in various capacities in the finance department of OIC International including Finance Officer, Senior Finance Officer and Acting Finance Manager.

Mr. Faibil also worked as Finance Officer for MDM Pty – a mining construction company.

Solomon has Master of Science in Financial Management from the Amity University of India. Mr. Faibil is a Chartered Accountant and a member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants (ICA) – Ghana and International Institute of Certified Forensic Accountants (IICFA) USA.

Solomon is a detailed-oriented and organized individual who typifies professionalism and ability to manage multiple tasks at any given minute.

Integrity, ability to prioritize multiple-task and manage competing deadlines, analytical skills, strong organizational and critical thinking skills, strong drive to implement change and drive to continuously learn, improve and develop are some of Solomon's competencies and skills.

DR. GORDON TERKPEH SABUTEY: MEMBER

Dr. Gordon Terkpeh Sabutey is a lecturer of the Department of Business, Faculty of Education, Law and Business Studies, University for Development Studies (UDS), Tamale and Wa Campuses, Ghana; as well as Adjunct Lecturer of the Institute of Distance Learning, Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), Kumasi, Ghana; KNUTSFORD University College, Legon, Accra. He is also the External Examiner for Jackson Educational Complex (JEC), Teacher Training Department, Ghana. He is a Resource Person to renowned organizations.

Dr. Sabutey holds degrees in PhD & MPhil in Art Education, MBA in Marketing & Corporate Strategy, and B.A. in Industrial Art from KNUST. His areas of specialization and research interest (lecturing) are Marketing Management & Planning, International Marketing, International Business, Corporate Strategy and Policy, Services Marketing & Customer Management, E-Marketing, Entrepreneurship, Management of NGOs, Innovation, Aesthetics & Criticism of Art as well as Psychology & Therapy of Art.

He lectures several courses at the post graduate and undergraduate levels as well as successfully supervised both post graduate and under graduate research works, UDS, KNUST and KNUTSFORD University College. He is a member of the Institute of Chartered Economists of Ghana (ICEG). Dr. Sabutey is also a member of other committees in the University.




DR. MICHELLE AFRIFAH: MEMBER

Dr. Michelle Afrifah is the Head of the Office of Student Research (OSR) at the Graduate School of the Ghana Technology University College. She holds a First Class Bachelor’s degree in Fine Arts from the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, an MSc in Tourism, Development and Policy from the University of Exeter in Devon, UK and a PhD in Human Geography from King’s College London. Her primary research interests include tourism and development, homeland development in relation to the African Diaspora, livelihoods of vulnerable actors, and gender in cultural history. Other areas of interest include the archaeological history of Ghana and how socio-cultural issues have played a role in the survival of our historical relics. Prior to joining GTUC, she was a lecturer at the College of East London, a teaching assistant at King’s College London and at KNUST. She also worked with JICA (Japan International Corporation Agency) as an administrative specialist under the Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture (formerly known back then as the Ministry of Tourism and Diaspora Relations). As a qualitative researcher, Dr. Afrifah believes in being rigorous and thorough when examining underlying causes and opinions of a society. She encourages intense and rigorous research that explores varied issues and provides valuable insight into important matters that can ultimately contribute significantly to key challenges facing our society.
